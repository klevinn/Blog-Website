About Manga and Light Novels

A Short summary of what Manga and Light Novels are. Manga is basically just a Japanese comic, packed with drawings depicting the story of the Anime , normally their stories go further than the Anime. This is because Animes are basically the adaptations of Mangas most of the time. Light Novels on the other hand are story books that depict the story, having very few illustrations throughtout the whole series. As previously mentioned, Light Novels are adapted into Mangas and Animes.

Manga

About the manga

The Manga , just like the Anime, is based of the Light Novel.
Info About the Manga

  • Volumes : 8
  • Chapters : 49 (So far only 16 have been translated to English)
  • Authors : Houki Kusano (Art) , Hajime Kamoshida (Story)
  • Serialised by Dengeki G's Comics in April 2011
  • From Memory, the story(Up till Chapter 16) ends quite early as compared to the Anime.
    It also is no longer being translated. There are no official translators if I'm not mistaken, however
    for most Manga Sites, the company ForgottenScans are the translators.
    They have not translated the manga since chapter 16.

Personal Opinion On Manga

This is mainly a comparison towards the anime. The manga is more of a budget / not produced as well version of the Anime. The standard of the anime was much higher than the standard of the manga. The abrupt stop in updates of the chapters will also leave non-anime watchers in the dark, which forces them to watch the Anime. To some that might be inconvenient or they just will not enjoy it as much as some people prefer Manga over Anime.

Light Novel

About the Light Novel

The Light Novel is the primary source of the Sakurasou Story!
Info about the Light Novel

  • Volumes : 10
  • Most Volumes have 4-5 Chapters of story.
  • The Anime ends at Volume 6 of the Light Novel. Volume 7-10 tells the rest of the story
  • Authors : Hajime Kamoshida (Story) , Keji Mizoguchi(Art/Illustrations)
  • Fully Released by ASCII Media Works as of October 2012.
  • Volumes 7 - 10 are not officially translated but there are many sites out there that translate it for you. Though some having quality that is not up-to-par, I recommend using jnovels. The English may not be very good, but throughout reading it, I had a very enjoyable time and it was still easily understandable.

Personal Opinion On the Light Novel

I did read a fan translated version of the Novel. However, if you enjoy the Anime. I recommend reading the Light Novel but not highly. It does talk more about the story and provides a more in depth view of the characters. Though as compared to the anime, I do believe that the standard might be a bit lower. Volumes 7 - 10, the story does become a bit dodgy / below standard as they focused a bit more on Fan-Service. Fan Service is material that is added to please the audience, mainly sexually   The Light Novel does also make the story from Volume 7-10 repetitive and not really unique anymore. However, the ending was an emotional rollercoaster and does bring closure to the whole story. So despite having subpar content I do recommend the novel as I believe it's ending will be worth the read.
