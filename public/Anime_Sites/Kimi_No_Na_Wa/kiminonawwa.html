Distributor : Toho , Funimation

Synopsis

Mitsuha Miyamizu, a high school girl that lives in the countryside, yearns to live in the bustling city of Tokyo. Meanwhile in the city, Taki Tachibana lives a busy life as a male high school student while juggling his part-time job, studies and hopes for a future in architecture.
One day, Mitsuha awakens in a room that is not her own and suddenly finds herself living the dream life in Tokyo but in Taki's body! Elsewhere, Taki finds himself living Mitsuha's life in the humble countryside. They then realise that they switch bodies randomly when they sleep and wake up.
Kimi no Na wa. revolves around Mitsuha and Taki's actions, which begin to have a dramatic impact on each other's lives, weaving them into a fabric held together by fate and circumstance.

More about the story

It may seem like a weird fictional story at first but throughout the movie, there are many scenes that are able to tug at the viewer's heartstrings. The movie itself is full of many comedic and interesting scenes while also being able to tackle the emotional problems the characters were facing. The movie also had a beautiful ending that really was able to wrap the whole movie together.

Personal Opinion

This movie is worth the watch. It is one of if not my favourite Japanese movies I have watched. Like previously mentioned, this movie was able to pull at my heart strings. In my opinion, it had the perfect portrayal of the theme of distant romance and loss. The ending also did leave me wanting more , though I was disappointed to find out that there was no story afterwards published. Lastly they had the perfect use of music to trigger many emotions at many times. I have comments on the music as well, majority of it are instrumentals which is not really my forte, but about the few songs that actually involve singing , personally I would recommend listening to them as they are very good songs.
